## Vector Database Reflection

Traditional keyword search relies on exact word matching, which may fail when similar concepts are expressed using different words. In contract analysis, legal language often varies even when the meaning is similar. Keyword search may miss relevant clauses if the exact words are not used.

Vector databases use embeddings to represent text as numerical vectors capturing semantic meaning. This allows similarity search based on meaning rather than exact words.

Using embeddings, a system can find contract clauses that are semantically similar even if the wording differs. This improves accuracy in legal document analysis and helps lawyers quickly locate relevant clauses.

Therefore, vector databases combined with embeddings provide a much more effective approach for contract analysis compared to traditional keyword search.
