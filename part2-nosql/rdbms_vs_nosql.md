## RDBMS vs NoSQL

Relational Database Management Systems (RDBMS) store data in structured tables with fixed schemas and relationships between tables. They support ACID properties which ensure data consistency and reliability. Examples include MySQL and PostgreSQL.

NoSQL databases are designed for flexible and scalable data storage. They allow dynamic schemas and can store data in formats such as documents, key-value pairs, graphs, or wide columns. Examples include MongoDB and Cassandra.

RDBMS systems are suitable for applications that require structured data and complex relationships, such as banking systems. NoSQL databases are better suited for large-scale applications with rapidly changing data structures, such as e-commerce product catalogs and social media platforms.
